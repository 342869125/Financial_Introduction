📖 Abstract

The financial market is a typical low Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) setting, which can usually destabilize off-policy maximum-entropy methods like Soft Actor-Critic (SAC). Specifically, noisy state representations may produce unreliable Q-value estimates, and bootstrapping amplifies these errors—a failure mode we call the "Financial Entropy Trap".

In this paper, we propose FPQC-SAC, an efficient and plug-and-play SAC variant that places a compact and bounded Parameterized Quantum Circuit (PQC) before the actor and critic networks to constrain feature propagation at the representation level, rather than filtering raw inputs or regularizing Q-values after bootstrapping.

Notably, FPQC-SAC reduces the impact of extreme market fluctuations on Bellman target estimation, while trainable quantum entanglement preserves flexible cross-asset interactions. Empirical evaluations on real-world portfolio management tasks demonstrate that FPQC-SAC substantially enhances out-of-sample stability and cumulative returns by achieving a 66.89% relative gain in cumulative return over standard unconstrained SAC and outperforms the best continuous-control deep reinforcement learning baseline by ~27%.

📖 Motivation

1. Low-SNR Financial Markets

Financial markets are inherently low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) environments. Useful financial signals are often extremely weak, whereas short-term fluctuations, market microstructure noise, investor sentiment, and liquidity shocks are dominant. This makes models prone to fitting historical noise patterns, suffering severe performance degradation in out-of-sample testing.

2. The Financial Entropy Trap

The Actor-Critic architecture becomes significantly fragile in noise-dominant markets. Maximum-entropy RL methods like SAC explicitly maximize policy entropy to encourage exploration. However, entropy-driven stochastic exploration does not necessarily gather effective information—it may amplify noise-dominated update directions. This prevents the policy from stably capturing weak predictive signals, forming a self-reinforcing loop we call the "Financial Entropy Trap".

3. Limitations of Existing Approaches

Existing solutions face inherent limitations: Output-side regularization (clipped double-Q, ensemble critics) intervenes too late—once market noise has contaminated intermediate representations, posterior corrections can only provide after-the-fact mitigation. Input-side filtering (Kalman filtering, wavelet denoising) relies on strong handcrafted priors and often erases scarce valuable signals alongside the noise.

Key Insight: Neither approach fundamentally blocks the propagation of noise perturbations into value estimation while preserving weak financial signals. A representation-level solution is needed.

🏗️ Methodology Details

FPQC-SAC represents a paradigm shift in financial reinforcement learning by embedding a Parameterized Quantum Circuit (PQC) at the representation level. Instead of filtering raw inputs or regularizing Q-values after bootstrapping, we constrain feature propagation using quantum mechanical properties to naturally filter noise via complex hyperspherical interference.

🔹 Hybrid Quantum-Classical Architecture

The state space is compressed via a classical linear layer to a 7-dimensional vector, aligned with the qubit topology. Features are embedded into quantum Hilbert space via continuous Rx angle encoding. The unitary evolution utilizes L = 2 layers of circular CNOT entanglement and single-qubit rotations. The latent representation is derived from expectation values of Pauli-Z observables.

🔹 Quantum Entanglement & Interference

The circular CNOT chain diffuses local Brownian perturbations into a collective multi-body entangled state. Isotropic noise undergoes destructive interference upon measurement, while correlated drift signals experience constructive alignment. This treats noise and signal asymmetrically: scrambling isotropic noise while focusing coherent signals.

🔹 Variance Attenuation Property

The PQC fundamentally attenuates variance via hyperspherical phase cancellation. Under unitary evolution, state ρ is confined to a norm-preserving complex hypersphere. Isotropic phase variations lacking structural correlation undergo destructive interference, exponentially attenuating output variance relative to input noise intensity.

📈 Theoretical Mechanisms

The superior performance of FPQC-SAC stems from three interconnected quantum-inspired mechanisms that fundamentally alter how noise propagates through the learning pipeline.

1. Complex Phase Encoding

The Rx angle encoding maps classical real-valued inputs into complex exponential phases. This naturally separates signal from noise: the predictive drift μ is preserved as a directional phase translation, while the Brownian diffusion σdWt induces an exponential decay factor under expectation. Mathematically: E[eiω⊤Zt+Δt] = eiω⊤Zt · (Signal Phase) · (Diffusion Attenuation).

2. Entanglement-Driven Interference

The circular CNOT entanglement chain creates a structural discriminator. Unstructured noise is scattered into isotropic high-dimensional phase perturbations and undergoes destructive interference upon Pauli measurement. Meanwhile, correlated drift signals with temporal and cross-asset structure are aligned along principal directions via trainable rotations, experiencing constructive interference.

3. Variance Dissipation

Unlike classical bottlenecks (Tanh, LayerNorm) that merely clip extreme values, the PQC fundamentally attenuates variance. By confining states to a norm-preserving complex hypersphere, isotropic phase variations undergo destructive interference. The output variance Var(fPQC) is exponentially attenuated relative to input intensity, completely decoupling stability from the infinite-variation nature of raw market SDEs.

Causal Chain: Interference-driven bottleneck → Suppressed TD variance → Minimal Q-gap → Superior out-of-sample trading performance.

⚙️ Quantum Advantage & Ablations

1. Stability vs. Classical Bottlenecks

While classical bounded activations (Tanh, LayerNorm) achieve moderate stability, they cannot fully suppress noise while retaining weak predictive signals. FPQC-SAC achieves 12 orders of magnitude lower critic loss variance compared to unconstrained SAC (10¹³ → 8.19), while simultaneously delivering the highest returns (195.3% vs. 154-170% for classical methods).

2. Entanglement is Essential

Ablation studies reveal that removing CNOT entanglement gates causes performance to drop from 195.3% to 142.1% cumulative return. Without entanglement, noise remains isolated within single-qubit subspaces rather than being scattered into dimensions where destructive interference can occur. This confirms that quantum entanglement is not merely a computational artifact—it is the core mechanism enabling noise-signal separation.

3. Trainable Rotations Matter

Freezing the quantum rotation matrices (using fixed random angles) reduces returns to 128.5%, below even classical MLP baselines. This demonstrates that the trainability of the quantum circuit is crucial—static quantum feature maps cannot adapt to market regime shifts or align coherent signals along optimal measurement axes.
